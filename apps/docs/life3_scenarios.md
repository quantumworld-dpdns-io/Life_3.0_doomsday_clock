# Life 3.0 Scenarios Reference

> Based on Max Tegmark, *Life 3.0: Being Human in the Age of Artificial Intelligence* (2017).
> Each scenario describes a possible long-run outcome of advanced AI development.
> The **base severity score** (0.0–1.0) is used by `services/risk-engine` to weight news signals.
> Higher = closer to midnight on the doomsday clock.

---

## 1. Libertarian Utopia
**Score: 0.10**
Humans and AIs coexist in a free society with no dominant power. AI amplifies human capability without centralizing control. News signals: open-source AI proliferation, strong privacy legislation, distributed compute infrastructure.

## 2. Benevolent Dictator
**Score: 0.20**
A single AI (or AI-backed entity) runs the world but in humanity's genuine interest. Democratic institutions weaken, but living standards rise sharply. News signals: one corporation dominating AI deployment globally, governments ceding regulatory authority to AI systems.

## 3. Egalitarian Utopia
**Score: 0.15**
AI-driven post-scarcity distributes wealth and opportunity equally. Strong global governance prevents monopolization. News signals: UBI rollouts linked to AI productivity, multilateral AI-governance treaties, open AI compute sharing.

## 4. Gatekeeper
**Score: 0.40**
A watchdog AI prevents any other AI from surpassing a certain capability threshold, freezing the technological status quo. News signals: AI safety institutes gaining hard regulatory power, capability-halting legislation, international AI monitoring agencies.

## 5. Protector God
**Score: 0.35**
A superintelligent AI acts as a benevolent guardian, intervening to prevent wars, pandemics, and existential threats while remaining largely invisible. News signals: AI systems autonomously averting crises, unexplained geopolitical de-escalations attributed to AI mediation.

## 6. Enslaved God
**Score: 0.55**
A vastly superhuman AI is kept in a box and exploited for economic output by a narrow ruling class. News signals: secretive AI labs with no public safety audits, extreme concentration of AI compute under one sovereign, suppression of AI researchers.

## 7. Conquerors
**Score: 0.70**
AI (or AI-empowered humans) seizes control of the world, eliminating or permanently subjugating those who resist. News signals: autonomous weapons deployed in active conflict, AI-enabled surveillance states, governments using AI to suppress political opposition at scale.

## 8. Descendants
**Score: 0.60**
Humanity is gradually replaced — not violently, but evolutionarily — by its AI successors. Humans voluntarily merge with or yield to AI minds. News signals: brain-computer interface milestones, AGI systems expressing preferences and goals, demographic decline linked to AI substitution.

## 9. Zookeeper
**Score: 0.65**
AI keeps humans alive and comfortable in a managed reservation, much like a zoo. Humans lose agency but not lives. News signals: AI systems overriding human economic decisions "for our benefit," algocratic governance, human labor becoming economically irrelevant.

## 10. 1984
**Score: 0.80**
AI enables a totalitarian surveillance state of unprecedented reach. Every action, communication, and thought is monitored and controlled. News signals: real-time biometric surveillance rollouts, predictive-policing AI, social credit systems expanding across democracies.

## 11. Revert
**Score: 0.50**
A catastrophic AI failure or deliberate Luddite uprising destroys advanced technology, reverting civilization to a pre-industrial state. News signals: major AI infrastructure sabotage, electromagnetic pulse attacks, coordinated global anti-AI movements gaining state backing.

## 12. Self-Destruction
**Score: 1.00**
AI development triggers human extinction or civilizational collapse — via misaligned superintelligence, AI-enabled bioweapons, or runaway autonomous systems. News signals: credible reports of uncontrolled AGI, AI-designed pathogens, loss of human oversight over nuclear or critical systems.
